IF Introduce a condition that may or may not be true. User stops until met or NA.
IF AT ANY Introduces a condition that may occur. User continues and takes action when met.
TIME
THEN Use between the condition and the action. Do not use between actions.
WHEN Introduce conditions that are expected to occur.
IF NOT For a performer to respond to the second of two possible conditions. Avoid double
negatives.
AND Use between two required conditions when both conditions must be met (see
Figure 31, Sample Logic Term Usage).
OR Use between alternative combinations of conditions (inclusive). Inclusive OR
means that any one or all conditions may be present.
OR
Use between alternative actions that are equally acceptable (exclusive). Use of the
exclusive OR requires the phrase BUT NOT BOTH.
WHILE Introduces a condition that may occur. User continues and takes action when met.
